## Decisions made up front (the brief invited these as questions)

- **Sky-band intensity falloff: cosine.** The band runs from full strength
  at the polygon's far edge to zero at a depth determined by
  `waterReflectionStrength`. Cosine matches Fresnel-like reflectivity off a
  flat surface, falls off gently in the middle and steeper at the edges,
  reads as painterly rather than as a hard mask. Linear is uniform-looking,
  exponential is too steep (loses the gradient). Documented as a Decision
  Log entry.

- **Front-lit water shows no glitter.** The brief's design constraint
  ("Front-lit water (sun behind camera) shows no glitter; back-lit water
  (sun ahead, near horizon) shows the strongest glitter") wins over the
  "some real lakes do, very faint" hint. The constraint is the more
  recognisable visual signal — strong directional asymmetry reads as
  "water reflecting the sky", a 15 % faint glitter on front-lit water just
  reads as noise. Future PR can revisit if curation surfaces a need.
